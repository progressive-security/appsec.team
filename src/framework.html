ASF | Application Security Framework #AppSecTeam

Introduction

There are many different approaches which teach how to build secure application. You are reading one of them.

We do not assert that ASF is the best way to make your application secure. We do not pretend to lead appsec world. This is just an attempt to mix many years of practical application security experience with security practices.

If You are looking for authoritative approaches, we strongly recommend to start from well-known ones:

  • Microsoft SDL https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/securityengineering/sdl
  • OWASP SAMM https://owaspsamm.org
  • BSIMM https://www.bsimm.com

How does ASF differ from the others

  • focused on the application, not development or security
  • flat design (without nested layers that make it difficult to dive into the framework)
  • column is a direction, cell is a practice
  • different ways of grouping the practices (by maturity level, by ability to automate, etc)

ASF

Governance

Trainings

CTF, workshops, lessons, articles

  • For: QA, developers
  • Why: to improve practical experience in teams
  • Profit: the ability to find some vulns, more attentive team
Awareness

Cyber incidents overview, best practices, surveys

  • For: development teams
  • Why: to show that cyberattack are not myths
  • Profit: the ability to fear cyber threats instead of frivolous perception
KPI and Performance Management

Reports, internal surveys, analysis

  • For: leadership team
  • Why: to give teams opportunity to want improve security state (KPI is a powerful reason)
  • Profit: teams ask how to improve security instead of you try to prove that security is important
PoC Demonstrations

Demonstrations with a real or obviously impact

  • For: executives, product owners
  • Why: owners always want to make money, it is necessary to prove they can loose profit without security
  • Profit: application security posture and importance
